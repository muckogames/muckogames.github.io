# Digory Stories - Story Bible

## Purpose
Working document that tracks recurring characters, settings, logic, tone, and plot patterns from the improvised "Digory stories" so future stories can be generated consistently.

## Core Tone & Style
- Bedtime / spoken improvised storytelling energy.
- Fast-moving, associative, dreamlike logic.
- Frequent interruptions and course corrections.
- Real places mixed with nonsense events.
- Child suggestions are incorporated instantly.
- Mild peril quickly resolved.
- Domestic concerns mixed with epic scale events.
- Repetition of phrases and names is normal.

## World Rules
- Seattle house can be reached from sea through water-elevator logistics.
- Geography is real-ish but highly flexible.
- Characters travel instantly or implausibly fast.
- Animals talk and participate as equals.
- Technology may be futuristic, impossible, or accessed through overly complicated apps.
- Huge environmental changes can happen casually (oceans drained, fleets moved into storage).
- Storage via Tupperwares is a recurring comic mechanism.
- Vehicles of any kind may appear: buses, submarines, minivans, hang gliders, cranes.

## Recurring Motifs
- Yellow sleeping surfaces: hammock, mattress, mat.
- Food solves engineering or navigation problems.
- Child corrections can instantly alter physics or save continuity holes.
- Breakfast involving mice.
- Sudden group decision: "What should we do today?"
- Real travel hubs (docks, Gibraltar, Atlantic, Italy).
- An absurd obstacle appears after plans begin.
- Bureaucratic or technical side quest (apps, model numbers, scanners).
- Lunch interrupting adventure.
- Child objections become canon.
- Story ends with sleep.

- **Digory** appears in the games as a dog ally of Samster, helping scout, sniff out Dieb hideouts, and move contraband to the police.

- **Lekan** is a panda character in the games; this is probably the same or related to the oral-story name sometimes heard as Likan.
- **Hippo** is a recurring core Mucko character.
- **Captain Mucko** is a central Mucko-universe figure.
- **Nik** is a seal character.
- **Basil** is a keeper / caretaker type character.
- **Saturn V** is personified enough to function as a character in crossover settings.

- There is a broader **Dieb universe** with contraband, hideouts, burglars, police, alarms, and reclamation-style heists.
- **Burglar Alley / Burgler Alley** is a recurring location associated with Dieb activity.
- Contraband commonly includes a comic mix of treasure and everyday objects: toys, jewels, lunch, coins, games, trophies, and more.

- The Mucko game world comfortably mixes heist plots, travel plots, educational minigames, and character crossovers.

## Proposal: Digory Submarine / Dieb Game Concept

### Working title
**Digory Stories: Diebs for Good**

### Overall structure
A multi-stage arcade/adventure game combining submarine travel, stealth heists, Chip's Challenge-style room puzzles, and a final home-defense sequence.

### Player characters
- **Samster** controls the submarine sections. This fits his expert-operator / escape-artist energy.
- **Digory** controls the Don's mansion heist / Chip's Challenge-style interior levels.

### Stage 1: Submarine outbound route
- Player drives the yellow submarine across an underwater route toward the Don's hideout.
- Route includes Kraken's underwater city infrastructure.
- Underwater gas stations appear along the way, built from shipwrecks, cruise ships, Titanic-like structures, half-ships, and other salvaged vessels.
- To refuel, park at a station and hold **E** until the gas meter fills.
- Running out of gas is a failure state.
- Goal: reach the Don's hideout at the end of the syndicate route.

### Stage 2: Deeb from the Don
- Top-down room puzzle inspired by **Chip's Challenge**.
- Player controls **Digory**.
- Objective: collect about **12 containers** and **12 floppy disks** from the Don's house.
- Floppy disks function like chips in Chip's Challenge: collect enough to progress or finish.
- Rooms contain computers, floppy disks, containers, keypads, and passwords.
- Some rooms require keypad codes.
- Passwords are often found in one room and used in another.
- First room has no keypad; it introduces basic movement and collection.
- Later rooms can include moving sidewalks, locked doors, room numbers, codes, and other Chip's Challenge-like obstacles.
- Optional secret: find and deeb the Don's exploding cigar.

### Stage 3: Submarine escape
- Player returns to submarine; control switches back to **Samster**.
- The Don pursues in a destroyer boat.
- Destroyer uses sonar / depth-charge style attacks.
- When a sonar/depth-charge object begins falling, the player has a warning window before impact; pressing **A** at the right time avoids or counters it.
- Return trip is about 10 km, but the submarine has only about 3 km of gas at full tank, so refueling remains necessary.
- If the player stole the exploding cigar in Stage 2, it can be thrown upward at a destroyer to blow it up or disable it, easing the escape.

### Stage 4: Home lockdown
- After reaching home, player runs around the house locking doors and windows before the Don can get in.
- The Don circles outside and tries entries at a steady rate.
- Goal: lock all doors / entries in time.
- Optional phone action: call **Firelight Guy**, who arrives with a flamethrower and scares the Don away from one side of the house.
- Optional Easter eggs:
  - Crow computer lets player play **The Sims** briefly.
  - Fireplace Robot lights a fire.
  - Rugs can comically catch fire and be put out; house itself is not flammable.
- Add light Chip's Challenge-style puzzle elements inside the house.

### Ending
- Player reaches basement and climbs onto the yellow blanket.
- Digory goes to sleep.
- Score screen appears with points and star rating, in the style of other Mucko games.

### Design notes
- The game should feel like a playable Digory bedtime story: absurd, sincere, and mechanically simple.
- Use short stages, generous checkpoints, and readable controls.
- Preserve the rhythm: adventure → heist → chase → home safety → yellow blanket sleep.

- Child co-author whose interjections rapidly redirect plot, canon, and physics.

## Operational Notes for Game/Creative Agents
- Preserve Digory as a real dog placed in absurd adventures, not a generic humanized mascot.
- Comedy comes from sincerity, not irony.
- Child logic should override realism when needed.
- Real places + impossible systems = core formula.
- Crow technology elegantly resolves timeline conflicts.
- Yellow sleeping surfaces are symbolic home/safety motif.
- Distinguish canons: Digory Stories, Mucko/Hippo line, but allow crossover cameos.

## Continuity Notes
- Repo canon suggests Digory may also exist as or alongside a dog-like character design associated with Pflueger's household; oral-story Digory and game Digory should be treated as closely related unless future stories separate them.
- Repo canon strongly confirms the spelling **Duck Dieb** and supports **Swan Dieb** as the same naming pattern.
- "Deeb" appears to mean stealing from thieves or reclaiming loot, often framed mischievously rather than maliciously.
- Keep names flexible if spoken variants occur.
- Preserve child interjections when possible.
- Logic should feel confident even when impossible.
- Stakes should stay playful, never frightening.
